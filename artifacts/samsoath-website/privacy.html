Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 2026

At Sam's OATH, your privacy is paramount. We understand the sensitive nature of the issues we address. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, protect, and share your information.

1. Information We Collect

When You Take Sam's OATH

We collect:

  • Identity display preference (full name, first name only, initials, or anonymous)
  • City and State
  • OATH category (for someone I love / in memory / supporter)
  • Email address (optional)
  • Date of pledge

What we display publicly: Your chosen identity display, city, state, and category appear as a pin on our map. Email is NEVER shown publicly.

When You Share Your Story

We collect:

  • Your name or chosen pseudonym
  • Email address (for communication only)
  • Your story content
  • Location (optional)

What we display publicly: Only what you explicitly approve. Stories are reviewed before publication, and you can request changes or removal anytime.

Automatically Collected Information

Like most websites, we automatically collect:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Device information
  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Referring website

This helps us improve our website and understand how people use it.

Cookies

We use cookies for:

  • Remembering your preferences
  • Analytics (understanding traffic patterns)
  • Ensuring website functionality

You can disable cookies in your browser, but some features may not work properly.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • Display your pin on the map (city, state, category only)
  • Send you a welcome email and resources (if you opt in)
  • Publish your story (with your explicit approval)
  • Send occasional updates about Sam's OATH (you can unsubscribe anytime)
  • Improve our website and services
  • Respond to your inquiries
  • Generate anonymized statistics (e.g., "1,247 people have taken the OATH")

3. What We DON'T Do

We will NEVER:

  • Sell your information to third parties
  • Share your email address publicly
  • Share your full name without your permission
  • Use your information for commercial marketing
  • Share your story without your explicit approval
  • Contact you excessively (you can unsubscribe anytime)

4. How We Protect Your Information

We implement security measures including:

  • SSL encryption for data transmission
  • Secure database storage
  • Limited access to personal data (only authorized team members)
  • Regular security reviews

However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Your Rights & Choices

You have the right to:

Access Your Information

Request a copy of the personal information we have about you.

Correct Your Information

Update or correct inaccurate information.

Delete Your Information

Request deletion of your information from our database. This will:

  • Remove your pin from the map
  • Remove your story from the website (if published)
  • Remove you from our email list

Opt Out of Communications

Unsubscribe from emails at any time using the link in any email we send.

Modify Your Story

Request changes or removal of your published story.

To exercise these rights, contact us at: privacy@samsoath.org

7. Children's Privacy

Sam's OATH is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such information, please contact us immediately.

8. California Privacy Rights

California residents have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to know what personal information is collected
  • Right to delete personal information
  • Right to opt out of sale of personal information (we don't sell information)
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising these rights

9. International Users

If you are accessing this website from outside the United States, please be aware that your information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in the United States. By using our website, you consent to this transfer.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes by:

  • Posting the new policy on this page
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date
  • Sending an email if the changes materially affect how we use your information
