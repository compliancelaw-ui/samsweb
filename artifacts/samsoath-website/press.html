About Sam's OATH

One-Sentence Summary:
Sam's OATH is a movement for families affected by a loved one's substance use or mental health challenges, committed to breaking stigma through openness, authenticity, togetherness, and healing.

Full Boilerplate:
Sam's OATH was founded by Frank Sheeder after the death of his son, Samuel, from a tragic accident in September 2025. Sam faced substance use disorder with courage and honesty, teaching his family that "the opposite of addiction is not sobriety, but community." When Frank shared Sam's story on LinkedIn, it reached 345,000 people and sparked thousands of responses from families hiding their own struggles. Sam's OATH (Openness, Authenticity, Togetherness, Healing) is a pledge to stop hiding and start connecting. The movement includes a public map of oath-takers, a story-sharing platform, workplace certification programs, and resources for families nationwide.
