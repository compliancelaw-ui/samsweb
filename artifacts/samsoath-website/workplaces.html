What is a Safe Listener?

Not HR. Not a therapist. Not a manager. A trained peer.

✅ Safe Listeners ARE:

  • Trained employees who volunteer to support colleagues
  • People who listen without judgment or advice-giving
  • Connectors to professional resources when needed
  • Living proof that "you're not the only one"
  • Bound by confidentiality (with safety exceptions)

❌ Safe Listeners are NOT:

  • Therapists or counselors
  • HR representatives or managers
  • There to solve problems or give advice
  • Required to report to leadership (unless safety risk)
  • Tracking or documenting conversations

What it looks like in practice:

Scenario:

An employee's teenage son just entered rehab. They're overwhelmed, exhausted, and considering taking FMLA but don't want colleagues to know why.

Safe Listener Response:

"I'm so glad you told me. My sister went through something similar last year—I know how isolating it feels. You're not alone in this. Have you looked into your EAP benefits? They have counselors who can help you navigate everything. And if you ever just need someone to listen, I'm here. No judgment."

Result: Employee feels seen, supported, and connected. They use EAP. They stay engaged at work. They don't quit.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is this different from our EAP?

EAPs are professional counseling services—critical but often underutilized because employees don't know they exist or feel uncomfortable using them. Safe Listeners are peers, not professionals. They're the first conversation, the "me too" moment, the bridge TO your EAP and other resources. They complement, not replace, professional support.

Won't this open us up to liability?

No. Safe Listeners are trained to listen and connect, not counsel or solve. They know when to refer to HR, EAP, or 911. We provide clear guidelines and documentation. In fact, proactive support reduces liability by preventing crises and demonstrating duty of care.

What if no one wants to be a Safe Listener?

In our experience, this is never the problem. The problem is too many people wanting to help. When you give employees permission to support each other, they show up. We typically see 15-20% of organizations volunteer.

How much time does this take?

Safe Listener training is 4 hours (can be split into two 2-hour sessions). Ongoing time commitment varies but averages 2-4 hours per month—mostly informal conversations that happen naturally.

Can remote teams do this?

Absolutely. Training is virtual. Safe Listeners can connect via Slack, Teams, Zoom, or any platform your team already uses. Distance makes authenticity even more important.

What industries is this for?

All of them. We've worked with healthcare systems, tech companies, manufacturing plants, law firms, nonprofits, schools, and more. Stigma doesn't discriminate by industry.
